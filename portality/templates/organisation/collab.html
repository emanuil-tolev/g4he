Define Collaboration

To help you find the collaborators you are interested in, select the roles an organisation can have on a project which you regard to be a "collaboration"

 Lead Research Organisation - An organisation that is in receipt of project funding and is accountable for ensuring the money is spent in accordance with the agreed terms and conditions.
 Principal Investigator - An organisation who hosts the individual who takes responsibility for the intellectual leadership of the research project and for the overall management of the research. He/she will be the main contact for the proposal. This is almost always the same as the Lead Research Organisation.
 Co-Investigator - An orgnanisation who hosts the Co-investigator (Co-I), who assists the Principal Investigator in the management and leadership of the research project
 Co-Funder - An organisation that financially supports the project through the main funder. This relationship includes Joint Funding relationships. Joint funders are other research councils that contribute to the funding of the project.
 Project Partner - An external organisation that has formal role in the supporting the project (either in cash or kind). Project Partners are named in the project proposal.
 Resulting Collaboration - Collaboration relationship between a project and an organisation that has been declared as an outcome of the project by the Project Principal Investigator and/or Lead Research Organisation.
 Fellow - The organisation hosting a fellowship.
